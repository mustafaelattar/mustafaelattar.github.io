Experience

CTO & VP, Ramyro Inc. (2024–Now)

Leading the development and deployment of an AI-powered radiology operating system. Oversaw partnerships with global AI vendors, directed strategic product roadmap planning, and implemented scalable infrastructure for diagnostic imaging workflows across Africa and the Middle East.

Co-Founder, Intixel Inc. (2018–Now)

Co-founded Intixel to create explainable and reliable AI software for radiologists. Designed and validated deep learning algorithms for breast cancer screening, lung CT interpretation, and personalized diagnostics. Led the company’s clinical validation efforts and market penetration strategies.

Director of Artificial Intelligence Program, Nile University (2020–Now)

Established and currently manage the AI undergraduate program at Nile University. Designed curricula, supervised graduation projects in deep learning and medical AI, and secured strategic academic-industry collaborations. Mentored faculty and students toward advanced research and innovation.

Associate Professor, Artificial Intelligence Program, Nile University (2023–Now)

Conducting high-impact research in deep learning for medical imaging, leading funded projects in collaboration with healthcare institutions, and supervising master’s and PhD students. Teaching advanced courses in machine learning, convolutional networks, and medical image processing.

Group Head, Medical Imaging and Image Processing (MIIP) (2017–Now)

Founded and lead the MIIP research group, focusing on developing robust algorithms for cardiac MRI, CT angiography, and oncology imaging. Coordinated multi-disciplinary research and published extensively in top-tier journals and conferences such as MICCAI and ISBI.

Assistant Professor, AI Program, CIS, Nile University (2017–2023)

Delivered foundational and advanced courses in AI and computer vision. Contributed to curriculum development, supervised thesis work, and published research in biomedical image analysis and pattern recognition. Played a key role in growing the AI department’s reputation.

Head of R&D, Myocardial Solutions Inc. (2017–2018)

Led the research and development of cardiac MRI quantification tools for fibrosis, strain, and function analysis. Coordinated with clinical partners for validation, and optimized the software pipeline for commercial use. Integrated AI-based modeling for myocardial tissue analysis.

Postdoctoral Fellow, Netherlands Cancer Institute (2016–2017)

Conducted postdoctoral research on image-guided oncology interventions, focusing on treatment planning and tumor localization in radiotherapy. Developed algorithms for 3D image registration and supported clinical trials in image-based navigation systems.

PhD Researcher, Academic Medical Center, University of Amsterdam (2012–2016)

PhD research on quantitative image analysis techniques for planning transcatheter aortic valve implantation (TAVI). Developed landmark detection and annulus dynamics models from 4D CT data. Published multiple first-author papers in high-impact journals.

Senior R&D Engineer, Diagnosoft Inc. (2010–2012)

Developed semi-automated tools for cardiac MRI analysis in clinical software suites. Focused on left ventricular segmentation and myocardial function quantification. Worked closely with international collaborators on algorithm validation and regulatory submissions.

Research Assistant, MIIP Lab, Nile University (2008–2010)

Early research role in the Medical Imaging and Image Processing Lab. Worked on segmentation of cardiac MRI, object recognition, and retinal image analysis. Contributed to the foundation of a long-term academic and clinical research path.

Education

Ph.D. in Biomedical Engineering – Academic Medical Center, University of Amsterdam (2012–2016)

Thesis: “Quantitative image analysis for planning of aortic valve replacement”
Supervised by Prof. Ed van Bavel, Prof. Henk Marquering, and Prof. Jan Baan. This research focused on 4D CT-based modeling and landmark detection for preprocedural planning of TAVI, resulting in highly cited publications and clinical validation across European centers.

M.Sc. in Communication & Information Technology – Nile University (2008–2010)

Thesis: “Segmentation of Cardiac Magnetic Resonance Images”
Supervised by Prof. Nael Fakhry Osman and Prof. Ahmed Samir Fahmy. Pioneered a novel segmentation framework for cardiac MRI analysis using region-growing and deformable models. The project was funded by internal grants and contributed to early cardiac AI initiatives in Egypt.

B.Sc. in Systems & Biomedical Engineering – Faculty of Engineering, Cairo University (2003–2008)

Thesis: “Retinal Image Analysis Toolkit with Network Support”
Developed a toolset for ophthalmic image analysis and remote diagnostics. The project secured a top-5 placement in the national “Made in Egypt” competition and laid the foundation for future work in medical image informatics.
